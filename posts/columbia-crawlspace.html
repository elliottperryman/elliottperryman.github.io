Summer at Columbia Crawlspace and Construction

The summer after my senior year of high school, I found myself at Columbia Crawlspace and Construction, ready for a unique and challenging experience. The job involved digging trenches in crawlspaces, a task made even more demanding by the necessity of wearing a suit and mask to protect against insulation spray.

This experience taught me how difficult work is. This was strong motivation to go to college. I truly believe every office job is easier than roofing or digging trenches while on your hands and knees on gravel in the dark, just as I think my coworker Morel knows more about what it means to work hard than any financial analyst who tells me how long the hours are at some silly finance firm.
