Hello Everybody!

For my first proper post, I made a game that simulates a neutron scattering experiment. You can play the game [here](https://neutrons.streamlit.app). I still have more that I could do to make the game better, but I am at a stopping point. What I want to convey is an understanding of the experimental design problem. You have an experimental setup, assume all the models are correctly written (big assumption), assume you can fit the true parameter (simply false), and have the experimental controls. The goal is to then collect the data that gives the best precision on some parameters of interest. The problem is that you have fixed resources and must intuit where to go. Hopefully the game makes it clear that a quantitative investigation into this problem could be worthwhile, although you would be surprised how much resistance that generates.
